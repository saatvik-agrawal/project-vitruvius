Density Block Modeling Workshop

This workshop introduces participants to the concept of density in urban design and planning. Participants will engage in hands-on modeling to visualize and analyze different density levels and their implications on urban spaces.

What Youâ€™ll Need

  • Foam blocks, LEGO bricks, or other modular materials for modeling
  • Baseboards or grids for layout
  • Markers and labels for annotation
  • Reference materials on urban density concepts
  • Facilitator to guide discussions and provide feedback

Steps

  1. Introduction: Explain the concept of urban density and its role in city planning.
  2. Exploring Density: Show real-world examples of different density levels, from low-rise suburban areas to high-density urban cores.
  3. Model Construction: Participants build physical models representing different density configurations.
  4. Comparative Analysis: Groups compare models and discuss the advantages and challenges of each density type.
  5. Urban Implications: Discuss how density affects infrastructure, transportation, sustainability, and livability.
  6. Reflection: Wrap up with a discussion on how density modeling can be applied to real-world urban planning.

Best Practices

  • Encourage participants to experiment with various density levels.
  • Use real-world case studies to provide context.
  • Discuss trade-offs between density and quality of life.
  • Ensure collaboration and open discussion among participants.

Designer and Resident Series of Workshops

This workshop series focuses on participatory design for housing, bringing together designers and residents to collaboratively develop living spaces that reflect community needs and values.

Workshop Series Structure

1. Initial Exploration: Understanding Resident Needs

  • Objective: Identify key priorities and expectations for housing.
  • Activities:
    • Resident discussions about daily routines and living conditions.
    • Case studies on successful participatory housing projects.
    • Sketching exercises to visualize personal housing needs.

2. Discovery Process: Ideation and Vision-Building

  • Objective: Develop conceptual housing designs based on resident feedback.
  • Activities:
    • Brainstorming sessions to define shared values and themes.
    • Group sketching of ideal housing layouts.
    • Collaborative exercises to define spatial relationships and community spaces.

3. Prototyping: Creating Mockups and Models

  • Objective: Translate ideas into tangible prototypes.
  • Activities:
    • 3D modeling of proposed housing structures.
    • Physical mockups using scale models.
    • Resident feedback loops to refine designs.

Best Practices

  • Encourage open communication between designers and residents.
  • Use real-world examples to inspire design thinking.
  • Ensure iterative feedback to refine concepts at each stage.
  • Document findings and proposals for future project implementation.

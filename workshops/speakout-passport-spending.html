Speakout Passport & Spending Workshop

This workshop allows participants to explore community priorities by engaging in a simulated budget allocation exercise using symbolic tax dollars and discussion-based decision-making.

What You’ll Need

  • A “Speakout Passport” for each participant
  • Several stalls, each representing a different discussion topic
  • Symbolic tax dollar tokens (paper or cards)
  • Boxes or containers for collecting tax dollars at each stall
  • Stamps or markers to mark each participant’s passport
  • A prize box for a concluding prize draw
  • Facilitator to oversee discussions and guide the process

Steps

  1. Introduction: Explain the objectives of the workshop, emphasizing the role of community input in budgeting and decision-making.
  2. Setting Up Stalls: Each stall represents a different community issue (e.g., education, transportation, infrastructure, healthcare).
  3. Discussion at Stalls: Participants visit stalls, engage in discussions, and receive a stamp in their Speakout Passport for each stall they visit.
  4. Budget Allocation: After visiting the stalls, participants receive symbolic tax dollars and distribute them among the stalls based on their priorities.
  5. Suggestion Notes: Participants can write specific feedback on the back of their tax dollars before depositing them in the stall boxes.
  6. Prize Draw: Participants tear out the last page of their stamped passport and place it in a prize box for a concluding draw.
  7. Reflection: Conclude by analyzing how tax dollars were distributed and discussing key insights from the workshop.

Best Practices

  • Encourage participants to engage in meaningful discussions before allocating their tax dollars.
  • Make the event interactive and engaging with well-structured discussion prompts.
  • Ensure transparency in how results from the tax dollar distribution will be used or analyzed.
  • Provide a mix of serious and lighthearted stalls to maintain engagement.

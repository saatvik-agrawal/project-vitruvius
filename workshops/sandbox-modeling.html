Sandbox Modeling Workshop

This workshop engages participants in hands-on modeling activities using sandbox techniques to explore spatial design, planning concepts, and environmental interactions.

What Youâ€™ll Need

  • Sandbox or large trays filled with sand
  • Miniature objects such as trees, buildings, and vehicles
  • Sticks, rulers, or other tools for shaping the sand
  • Reference images for inspiration
  • Facilitator to guide discussions and encourage creativity

Steps

  1. Introduction: Explain the purpose of sandbox modeling and how it aids in visualizing and experimenting with design ideas.
  2. Scenario Definition: Participants define the scenario or theme they want to model, such as urban design, landscape planning, or disaster response.
  3. Model Creation: Participants use the sandbox and miniature objects to build their models, experimenting with different layouts and configurations.
  4. Analysis and Discussion: Groups analyze their models, discussing spatial relationships, challenges, and potential improvements.
  5. Iteration and Refinement: Based on feedback, participants refine their models, making adjustments to improve functionality or representation.
  6. Presentation: Each group presents their final models and explains their design choices.
  7. Reflection: Conclude with a discussion on the applications of sandbox modeling in real-world problem-solving.

Best Practices

  • Encourage hands-on experimentation with different model configurations.
  • Use diverse materials to create varied textures and elements in the sandbox.
  • Facilitate discussions to draw connections between models and real-world challenges.
  • Document and photograph final models for future reference.

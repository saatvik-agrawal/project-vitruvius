A Week with a Camera Workshop

This workshop focuses on using photography as a tool for observation, reflection, and storytelling. Participants will capture images over the course of a week to document their experiences, surroundings, and perspectives.

What Youâ€™ll Need

  • A camera or smartphone with a camera
  • Notebook or digital journal for reflections
  • Computer for organizing and editing images
  • Projector or display for showcasing final work
  • Facilitator to guide discussions and provide feedback

Steps

  1. Introduction: Discuss the purpose of using photography for documentation and storytelling.
  2. Assignment Brief: Participants receive guidelines on capturing images related to a chosen theme (e.g., daily life, community, architecture).
  3. Photography Phase: Over the course of a week, participants take photos that reflect their observations and experiences.
  4. Journaling: Participants write short reflections on their images, describing the context and emotions behind each capture.
  5. Photo Selection: Participants curate a selection of their best photos for presentation.
  6. Presentation and Discussion: Participants share their curated images and discuss their insights.
  7. Reflection: Conclude with a discussion on the role of visual documentation in storytelling and analysis.

Best Practices

  • Encourage participants to experiment with different angles, lighting, and compositions.
  • Ensure ethical considerations in photography, including privacy and consent.
  • Use a mix of candid and staged photography to capture diverse perspectives.
  • Encourage open discussions to enhance storytelling through images.

Mapping Activity Workshop

This workshop helps participants visualize and analyze community spaces by mapping areas of importance, concern, and potential improvement. Mapping activities encourage discussion on spatial organization and community needs.

What Youâ€™ll Need

  • Large maps or printed layouts of the community or focus area
  • Colored markers, pens, and sticky notes
  • Reference materials for geographical or historical context
  • Facilitator to guide discussions and coordinate mapping

Steps

  1. Introduction: Explain the purpose of the mapping activity and its role in community planning.
  2. Defining Key Locations: Participants identify significant spaces such as landmarks, gathering areas, and neglected zones.
  3. Marking Areas of Interest: Participants use markers and sticky notes to highlight areas they value, need improvement, or are underutilized.
  4. Discussion and Analysis: Groups discuss their findings, identifying patterns, gaps, and opportunities.
  5. Proposing Solutions: Participants brainstorm and document ideas for improving mapped areas.
  6. Reflection: Wrap up with a discussion on how mapping informs community planning and engagement.

Best Practices

  • Encourage diverse perspectives by including various community members.
  • Use different colors for mapping different elements (e.g., green for positive spaces, red for problem areas).
  • Ensure an open and inclusive discussion environment.
  • Document the final maps for future reference and action planning.

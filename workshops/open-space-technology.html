Open Space Technology Workshop

The Open Space Technology (OST) workshop is a self-organized discussion format where participants set the agenda and lead conversations. This method is ideal for large groups looking to explore complex topics in a collaborative way.

What Youâ€™ll Need

  • A large open space for breakout discussions
  • Flip charts, whiteboards, or large sheets of paper
  • Markers and pens for participants
  • Facilitator to guide the initial setup and closing reflection

Steps

  1. Introduction: Explain the purpose and principles of Open Space Technology.
  2. Agenda Creation: Participants propose topics and write them on a board or flip chart.
  3. Breakout Sessions: Participants self-organize into discussion groups based on their interests.
  4. Discussion: Each group explores its topic while one person documents key takeaways.
  5. Sharing Insights: Groups reconvene and share summaries of their discussions.
  6. Reflection: The facilitator leads a closing session to highlight common themes and next steps.

Best Practices

  • Encourage an open and flexible structure where participants take ownership of discussions.
  • Ensure that all voices are heard by promoting active listening.
  • Allow participants to move between groups if they feel they can contribute more elsewhere.
  • Document all insights and discussions for future reference.

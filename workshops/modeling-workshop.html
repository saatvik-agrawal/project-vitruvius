Modeling Workshop

This workshop focuses on hands-on modeling techniques to explore design concepts, spatial arrangements, and structural relationships. Participants will engage in creative problem-solving through physical and digital modeling exercises.

What You’ll Need

  • Modeling materials (e.g., clay, foam, cardboard, 3D printing resources)
  • Cutting tools, glue, and other assembly supplies
  • Sketching materials for design planning
  • Computers with 3D modeling software (optional)
  • Facilitator to guide discussions and exercises

Steps

  1. Introduction: Explain the purpose of modeling and its applications in design and problem-solving.
  2. Concept Development: Participants brainstorm and sketch preliminary ideas before creating models.
  3. Material Selection: Discuss the properties of different modeling materials and choose appropriate ones.
  4. Hands-on Modeling: Participants build models individually or in groups, refining their designs as they progress.
  5. Presentation and Feedback: Groups present their models, discuss challenges, and receive constructive feedback.
  6. Reflection: Conclude with a discussion on the effectiveness of modeling in visualizing concepts and problem-solving.

Best Practices

  • Encourage iterative design—participants should refine their models based on feedback.
  • Use a mix of physical and digital modeling tools for a comprehensive experience.
  • Discuss real-world applications of modeling in engineering, architecture, and product design.

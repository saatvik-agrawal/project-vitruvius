Picturization Exercise Workshop

This workshop helps participants visualize ideas and concepts using symbols and pictorial representations. By engaging in a structured exercise, participants can better communicate thoughts and priorities regarding community planning and design.

What You’ll Need

  • A selection of symbols representing different community elements (e.g., trees, benches, schools, roads, parks, etc.)
  • Large sheets of paper or poster boards
  • Markers, pens, and sticky notes for annotations
  • A facilitator to guide discussions and structure the exercise

Steps

  1. Introduction: Explain the goal of picturization and how visual representations help in expressing community needs.
  2. Symbol Selection: Participants choose from a variety of symbols to represent what they want or do not want in a community project.
  3. Placement on Board: Participants place their selected symbols onto large sheets of paper, forming a visual layout of their ideal community space.
  4. Annotations and Descriptions: Participants write brief explanations next to their symbols, detailing their choices and priorities.
  5. Group Discussion: Participants walk around, review each other’s picturization layouts, and engage in discussions about common themes and differences.
  6. Final Round: The group comes together to synthesize insights and reflect on recurring patterns and unique perspectives.
  7. Reflection: Conclude with a discussion on how picturization can be applied to community engagement and participatory planning.

Best Practices

  • Encourage creativity—participants can combine or modify symbols to better reflect their ideas.
  • Ensure a relaxed and interactive atmosphere to promote open discussion.
  • Use diverse visual elements to capture different aspects of community needs.
  • Document and photograph the final picturization boards for future reference.

Design Scenarios Workshop

This workshop engages participants in solving real-world design problems using structured case studies and collaborative problem-solving techniques.

What Youâ€™ll Need

  • Case studies and design challenges relevant to the workshop theme
  • Whiteboards, large sheets of paper, or digital collaboration tools
  • Markers, pens, and sticky notes for brainstorming
  • Facilitator to guide discussions and problem-solving exercises

Steps

  1. Introduction: Explain the purpose of design scenarios and their role in tackling real-world challenges.
  2. Presenting Challenges: Participants are introduced to a design scenario, detailing its constraints, stakeholders, and objectives.
  3. Mapping Solutions: Using provided case studies, participants analyze previous approaches and identify key takeaways.
  4. Group Analysis and Ideation: Teams brainstorm possible solutions, leveraging past case studies and innovative thinking.
  5. Prototyping (Optional): If applicable, participants sketch or model their proposed solutions.
  6. Presentation: Each group presents their design scenario solutions and receives feedback.
  7. Reflection: Conclude with a discussion on how these methods can be applied in real-world design practice.

Best Practices

  • Encourage participants to think critically and challenge assumptions.
  • Use real-world case studies to provide practical context.
  • Ensure that all participants contribute to group discussions.
  • Document the process and solutions for future reference.

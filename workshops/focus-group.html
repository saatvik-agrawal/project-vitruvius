Focus Group Workshop

The Focus Group workshop is designed to gather in-depth insights from participants through guided discussions. This format helps explore opinions, experiences, and attitudes about a specific topic.

What Youâ€™ll Need

  • A quiet and comfortable meeting space
  • 6-10 participants for diverse perspectives
  • A prepared list of open-ended discussion questions
  • A facilitator to guide the conversation
  • Notebooks, pens, or recording devices to document responses

Steps

  1. Introduction: Explain the purpose of the focus group and set ground rules for discussion.
  2. Questioning: The facilitator asks open-ended questions to encourage discussion among participants.
  3. Encouraging Participation: Ensure that all voices are heard and balanced within the discussion.
  4. Follow-up Probing: Ask clarifying or deeper questions to expand on key points raised by participants.
  5. Documentation: Record or take notes on the discussion for analysis.
  6. Summary and Reflection: Conclude by summarizing key takeaways and thanking participants for their input.

Best Practices

  • Create a comfortable, non-judgmental environment to encourage honest responses.
  • Use neutral language to avoid influencing participant responses.
  • Facilitate rather than dominate the discussion.
  • Analyze responses for patterns and actionable insights.

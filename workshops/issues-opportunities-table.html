Issues/Opportunities Table Workshop

This workshop facilitates the identification of key issues and opportunities within a given project or community setting. Participants collaborate to create a structured table that organizes challenges and potential solutions.

What Youâ€™ll Need

  • Large sheets of paper or whiteboards
  • Markers, sticky notes, or index cards
  • Reference materials relevant to the topic
  • Facilitator to guide discussions and ensure structure

Steps

  1. Introduction: Explain the purpose of the Issues/Opportunities Table and how it can be used to analyze challenges and identify solutions.
  2. Topic Definition: Define the scope of the discussion, ensuring participants understand the focus area.
  3. Brainstorming Session: Participants generate a list of issues related to the topic and write them on sticky notes or index cards.
  4. Categorization: Organize the issues into thematic groups and document them in a structured table format.
  5. Opportunity Identification: Participants propose potential solutions or opportunities for each identified issue.
  6. Discussion and Refinement: Groups discuss proposed solutions, refine ideas, and evaluate feasibility.
  7. Presentation: Teams present their tables to the larger group for feedback and additional insights.
  8. Reflection: Conclude with a discussion on how this method can be applied to real-world planning and decision-making.

Best Practices

  • Encourage active participation and collaboration.
  • Ensure a balanced approach by considering both challenges and opportunities.
  • Use color coding or symbols to visually distinguish different categories.
  • Facilitate open-ended discussion to capture diverse perspectives.

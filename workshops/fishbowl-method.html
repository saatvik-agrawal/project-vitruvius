Fishbowl Method Workshop

This workshop uses the Fishbowl Method, a structured discussion format that allows participants to engage in meaningful conversations while maintaining an inclusive environment.

What Youâ€™ll Need

  • A large open space with chairs arranged in concentric circles
  • A topic or question to guide the discussion
  • A facilitator to oversee the session
  • Note-taking materials for participants

Steps

  1. Setup: Arrange chairs in a small inner circle (3-5 seats) and a larger outer circle for observers.
  2. Introduction: Explain the Fishbowl Method rules and goals.
  3. Start the Discussion: A few participants sit in the inner circle and start the conversation while others listen.
  4. Rotation: When someone from the outer circle wants to contribute, they tap a seated participant to swap places.
  5. Facilitation: Ensure smooth transitions, keep the discussion on track, and encourage active listening.
  6. Reflection: After the discussion, gather feedback from participants about their experience.

Best Practices

  • Choose a thought-provoking topic relevant to the group.
  • Encourage participation by creating a safe and respectful space.
  • Allow time for reflection and feedback at the end of the session.

Visual Preference Survey Workshop

This workshop allows participants to evaluate different visual elements in community planning and design. By rating and discussing various images, participants identify preferences that guide planning and decision-making.

What Youâ€™ll Need

  • A selection of photographs or illustrations representing different urban and public spaces
  • Rating sheets or an online survey platform for collecting responses
  • Markers, pens, or digital devices for participants to provide feedback
  • Facilitator to guide discussions and ensure structured evaluation

Steps

  1. Introduction: Explain the purpose of the visual preference survey and how it informs planning decisions.
  2. Displaying Images: Present images of different urban designs, streetscapes, public spaces, and building styles.
  3. Individual Rating: Participants score each image based on appeal, usability, and overall preference.
  4. Discussion and Analysis: Groups discuss their ratings, explaining preferences and highlighting key design elements.
  5. Identifying Trends: Facilitators compile responses to identify common themes and preferences.
  6. Application of Findings: Discuss how survey results can influence future urban planning and community projects.
  7. Reflection: Wrap up with a discussion on the importance of aesthetics and functionality in public spaces.

Best Practices

  • Use a diverse selection of images to capture various design styles and urban settings.
  • Encourage open discussion and differing viewpoints.
  • Ensure accessibility by providing digital and physical rating options.
  • Use survey results to develop actionable recommendations for future projects.
